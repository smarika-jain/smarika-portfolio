## The Problem

Responding to LinkedIn messages manually takes hours daily. Worse, escalation cases (complaints, refunds, legal issues, bug reports) get either missed or auto-replied to with generic responses, damaging customer relationships at exactly the moments that matter most.

Teams need automation that handles the routine while protecting the sensitive.

## What I Built

A reply automation workflow that monitors LinkedIn inboxes via the HeyReach API and routes incoming messages through a decision tree before deciding whether to auto-respond.

### How It Works

1. **Monitor inboxes.** Workflow polls HeyReach for new LinkedIn messages and retrieves full conversation history (not just the latest message).

2. **Escalation detection.** Each message is scanned for escalation keywords (complaint, refund, error, legal, security, bug, etc.). Matches get routed to the human team. No auto-reply fires.

3. **FAQ retrieval.** For non-escalation messages, the workflow fetches relevant FAQs from a Google Sheets knowledge base.

4. **Personalized reply generation.** GPT-4 generates a context-aware reply using: full conversation history, relevant FAQs, and the lead's profile data. Tone matches the lead's communication style.

5. **Send and log.** Reply goes out via LinkedIn. All interactions are logged to a Google Sheet for auditing and pattern review.

### Key engineering decisions

- **Full conversation context** sent to GPT-4, not just the latest message (responses make sense in conversation flow)
- **Whitelist over blacklist for keywords**: escalation keywords are explicit; everything else goes to auto-reply (better to over-escalate than miss a sensitive case)
- **Audit log** for every reply (clients can review what went out and adjust the FAQ accordingly)
- **Tone matching**: if the lead writes casually, the reply is casual; if they write formally, the reply matches
