## The Problem

Raw lead lists arrive messy. Job titles vary wildly (CFO, Chief Financial Officer, Head of Finance, Director of Finance all mean the same thing). Locations come as unstructured strings ("Mumbai, MH, India" vs "Bombay" vs "Mumbai India"). Without normalization, downstream segmentation falls apart.

SDRs end up manually tagging job functions and parsing locations row by row. The work is slow, inconsistent across team members, and prone to error.

## What I Built

A two-stage enrichment workflow that runs over the entire lead list.

### Stage 1: Job Title Classification

GPT-4 reads each job title and returns: a functional category (Sales, Operations, Tech, Finance, HR, Marketing, Founder, Investor, or Other), and a seniority tag (Junior, Mid, Senior, Executive).

The model handles synonyms (CFO and Chief Financial Officer both classify as Finance Executive), acronyms (CMO, CTO, CRO all classify correctly), and ambiguous titles (Head of Growth gets classified as Marketing Executive based on context).

### Stage 2: Location Enrichment & Priority Scoring

Raw location strings are parsed into structured fields: city, state, country. Each lead then receives a priority tier (Tier 1 = target first, Tier 2 = secondary, Tier 3 = lower priority) based on a configurable scoring rubric the client defines (typically a mix of geography, company size, and role seniority).

### Why This Design

Both stages run as separate n8n workflows that read from and write to the same Google Sheet. This means the client can re-run stage 2 (priority scoring) without re-running stage 1 (job title classification) when they want to adjust their priority rubric. Decoupled stages = faster iteration.

## The Results

Estimated impact for outbound teams running this workflow:

- **$150 saved per 1,000 leads** in manual SDR labor
- **20 to 40 hours saved per week** for teams handling hundreds or thousands of leads
- **Roughly 50K to 1L INR per month** in operational efficiency depending on team size
